The derivative of \(f\) is
\begin{equation*} f'(x)=3x^2+5x^4\cos\big(x^5\big)=x^2\left(3+5x^2\cos\big(x^5\big)\right) \end{equation*}
So, if \(f'(x)=0\text{,}\) then \(x=0\) or \(\left(3+5x^2\cos\left(x^5\right)\right)=0\text{.}\) If \(x \in (-1,0) \cup (0,1)\text{,}\) then
\begin{align*} |x|\lt 1 \amp\implies |x^5|\lt 1\lt \tfrac{\pi}{2} \implies \cos\big(x^5\big)>0 \\ \amp\implies 3+5x^2\cos\big(x^5\big) > 3 \\ \amp\implies f'(x) \neq 0 \end{align*}
That is, there is no \(c \in (-1,0) \cup (0,1)\) with \(f'(c)=0\text{.}\) Therefore, following our last bullet point, \(f(x)\) has only one root.
